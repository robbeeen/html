#1 The Bro Code.

In popular culture, the Bro Code is a friendship etiquette to be followed among men or, more specifically, among members of the bro subculture.
The term was invented and popularized by Barney Stinson, a character from the television show How I Met Your Mother.
Originally published: 14 October 2008
Author: Barney Stinson
Original language: English
Genre: Humour

















#2 The Adventures Of Sherlock Holmes.

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes is a collection of twelve short stories by Arthur Conan Doyle, first published on 14 October 1892.
Originally published: 14 October 1892
Publisher: George Newnes
Characters: Sherlock Holmes, John H. Watson, Irene Adler, MORE
Genres: Short story, Novel, Mystery, Detective novel, Noir fiction, Crime Fiction

















#3 The Notebook.

The Notebook is a 1996 romantic novel by American novelist Nicholas Sparks. The novel was later adapted into a popular film of the same name, in 2004.
Originally published: 1 October 1996
Author: Nicholas Sparks
Followed by: The Wedding
Page count: 214
Series: The Notebook and The Wedding
Characters: Noah, Jr., Allie Hamilton, Lon Hammond, Anne Hamilton, Dr. Barnwell

















#4 The Invisible Man.

The Invisible Man is a science fiction novel by H. G. Wells. Originally serialized in Pearson's Weekly in 1897, it was published as a novel the same year.
Originally published: 1897 Author: H. G. Wells
Publisher: Sir Arthur Pearson, 1st Baronet
Characters: Dr. Arthur Kemp, Hawley Griffin, Jenny Hall, Mr. Hall, Thomas Marvel
Adaptations: The Invisible Man (2020), The Invisible Man (1933), MORE
Genres: Novel, Science Fiction, Horror fiction, Scientific romance

















#5 Harry Potter.

Harry Potter is a series of seven fantasy novels written by British author, J. K. Rowling.
The novels chronicle the lives of a young wizard, Harry Potter, and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, all of whom are students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.
Author: J. K. Rowling
First book: Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone
Number of books: 7
Genres: Fantasy Fiction, Drama, Young adult fiction, Mystery, Thriller, Bildungsroman
Characters: Hermione Granger, Harry Potter, Draco Malfoy and MORE
